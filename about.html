Our Story

We're building the infrastructure for catalog-driven commerce

eCommerce operations are drowning in manual catalog work. We built SpikeList to eliminate it.

🎯

Mission

Simplify complex eCommerce operations through automation. Every hour your team spends on manual catalog work is an hour not spent on growth. We're here to reclaim that time.

🚀

Vision

Become the infrastructure layer for catalog-driven commerce. When a product enters a supply chain, SpikeList ensures it's instantly discoverable, accurately priced, and perfectly presented — on every platform where buyers exist.

The Problem We Solve

Catalog operations were never designed to scale

Most eCommerce teams are stitching together spreadsheets, FTP uploads, and one-off integrations to manage product data across dozens of suppliers and channels. The result is errors, delays, and a team perpetually stuck in operational firefighting rather than strategic work.

SpikeList was built by operators who lived this problem. We know exactly what breaks at 10,000 SKUs, at 50 suppliers, at 10 marketplace channels — and we built a system that handles it without friction.

How We Work

Principles that shape everything we build

⚡

Automation Over Configuration

If it can be automated, it will be. We don't add settings where logic can work. Every manual step is a future failure point.

🧱

Infrastructure Thinking

We build for reliability, not novelty. Catalog data is operational infrastructure — it needs to work every time, at any scale.

🔍

Operator-Level Depth

We understand the actual complexity of multi-vendor, multi-channel eCommerce. Our product reflects that depth, not a simplified version of it.

📐

Precision Over Feature Velocity

We'd rather ship one thing that works perfectly than five things that work most of the time. Quality of execution over quantity of releases.

🌐

Built for Global Commerce

Multi-currency, multi-language, multi-region — the eCommerce world is global. SpikeList is built to operate across borders without friction.

🤝

Partnership with Our Customers

Our customers aren't users — they're operations partners. Their catalog complexity shaped our product, and their success is our roadmap.
